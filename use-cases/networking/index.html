The Problem

Networking Groups Are Powerful. But Broken.

You're surrounded by people who want to help you. They just don't know how. It's not that they won't refer you. It's that giving referrals isn't top of mind when they're in conversations with the right people.

🎲

Referrals Are Random

You can't control when someone thinks of you. Most referral opportunities happen when your contact is in a conversation and you're nowhere near their mind.

🤷

"I Don't Know Who to Refer"

Your networking partners want to help. But when you ask "who do you know that needs X?" they draw a blank. The ask is too vague.

💸

Expensive With No System

BNI: $2,500/yr. Vistage: $15,000+/yr. Chamber memberships. Event tickets. Add it up and you're investing serious money with no referral system.

The Fix

Stop Asking "Who Do You Know?" Start Asking for Specific Names.

The number one reason networking groups underperform is vague asks. "Send me anyone who needs marketing" doesn't work. "Can you introduce me to Sarah Cohen at TechCo?" does.

The Inroad Engine maps every member's LinkedIn network. You see exactly who they're connected to. Now instead of hoping for referrals, you walk into your next meeting with a list of specific names to ask about.

Before & After

From "Does Anyone Know Someone?" to "Can You Intro Me to Sarah?"

Before the system: You walk into your BNI meeting and say "I'm looking for business owners in the $5M-$50M range who need marketing help." Everyone nods. Nobody follows up. Because the ask is too vague and they can't think of anyone on the spot.

After the system: You walk in and say "Tom, I see you're connected to Sarah Cohen at TechCo. She's exactly the kind of client I work with. Would you be open to making an intro?" Tom says "Oh yeah, Sarah! Great person. I'll text her right now."

That's the difference. Specific beats vague. Every time.
