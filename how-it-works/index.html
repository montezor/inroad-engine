How the Inroad Engine Works

A detailed look at how we analyze your relationships, score prospects, and surface warm introduction opportunities your team would never find manually.

1

You Share Your Centers of Influence

It starts with a simple question: who already trusts you? We call these people your Centers of Influence (COIs). They're your existing clients, referral partners, mentors, strategic allies, and business friends.

You don't need to export anything. Just give us their names and LinkedIn profiles. Most clients start with 5-10 COIs and expand from there.

  • →Current clients who love your work
  • →Referral partners in complementary industries
  • →Mentors, advisors, or peer group members
  • →Past colleagues who've moved to new companies
  • →Anyone who would happily introduce you if asked

We Map Their LinkedIn Engagement

This is where the intelligence happens. We don't just look at who your COIs are connected to. We analyze who they actually engage with on LinkedIn.

Comments, reposts, reactions, endorsements. These are signals of real relationships, not just a "connect" button click from 5 years ago. There's a massive difference between a LinkedIn connection and a real relationship.

  • →Comments & replies indicate active, two-way relationships
  • →Reposts & shares show respect and amplification
  • →Reactions (especially thoughtful ones) signal regular attention
  • →Endorsements & recommendations prove deep trust
  • →Frequency matters: engaging weekly vs. once a year tells a different story

3

AI Scores Every Prospect

Every person your COI engages with gets run through our scoring engine. We evaluate them across five dimensions to determine who's actually worth pursuing.

This isn't a vanity score. Each dimension is weighted based on how likely the intro will convert to a real conversation. A perfect ICP fit with zero warmth scores lower than a decent fit with a strong relationship.

  • 🎯ICP Fit (0-100): Does this person match your ideal customer profile? Industry, title, company size, geography.
  • 🔥Warmth (0-100): How strong is the actual relationship between your COI and this person? Based on engagement frequency and depth.
  • 👔Authority (0-100): Are they a decision-maker? Can they sign a check or do they need to "run it up the flagpole"?
  • 📊Activity (0-100): Are they actively posting and engaging on LinkedIn? Active people respond to intros faster.
  • 🌐Strategic Value (0-100): Even if they're not a buyer, would this person open doors to new markets, industries, or referral chains?

Everything Lands in Your Dashboard

All of this intelligence flows into your private dashboard. Every COI. Every scored prospect. Every warm path. Organized, searchable, and actionable.

The dashboard is designed for speed. You should be able to open it, find a high-priority prospect, and request an intro in under 60 seconds. No training required. No CRM certification.

  • →Accordion-style COI explorer. Click to expand any contact.
  • →Filter by score, industry, title, or status
  • →Color-coded priority: orange = high, yellow = medium, blue = standard
  • →Track outreach status: requested, skipped, meeting booked, closed
  • →New reports added daily. Your dashboard grows over time.

5

You Request the Intro. 30 Seconds.

Here's where it becomes revenue. You found a high-scoring prospect. You see the warm path. Now what?

Click the "Request Intro" button. A pre-written, personalized email opens in your inbox, addressed to your COI, asking for the introduction. The email is written to be natural, respectful, and effective. You can edit it or send as-is.

No templates to find. No awkward "hey, do you know anyone at...?" conversations. Just a clear, professional ask that makes it easy for your COI to say yes.

  • →Pre-written email includes the prospect's name, title, and company
  • →Explains why you want the intro (based on ICP match)
  • →Makes it easy for your COI: includes a forwardable blurb about you
  • →30 seconds from dashboard to sent email
  • →Track the status: requested → intro made → meeting booked → closed

Privacy & Safety

We never ask for your LinkedIn credentials. No bots running on your profile. No risk of LinkedIn restrictions. Zero.
